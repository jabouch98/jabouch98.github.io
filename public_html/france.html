Chateau de Chenonceau

This castle was built on the River Cher during the Renaissance. This castle is not only beautiful on the outside but the inside has a maze, woods, and a small farm that you can explore.

Chateau d'Angers

This castle is older than the Chateau de Chenonceau and the outside reflects that. However, once inside you can see how refined it is. It is home to the oldest intact medieval French tapestry which is 103 meters long.

Chateau de Bonaguil

This was one of the greatest feudal castles. It current appearance is from the early 1600s. This castle was home to seven drawbridges, an number of murder holes, and even gun port.
